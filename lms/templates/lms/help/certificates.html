🎓 Certificates

Learn about earning and managing your course certificates.

How to Earn a Certificate

To earn a certificate of completion, you must:

  1. Complete all required course materials
  2. Participate in course activities
  3. Submit all assignments and projects
  4. Pass all quizzes with minimum passing grade (typically 60%)
  5. Achieve an overall course grade of 60% or above

Upon meeting all requirements, your certificate will be generated automatically.

Certificate Requirements by Course

Different courses may have different requirements:

  • Beginner Courses: 60% overall grade required
  • Intermediate Courses: 70% overall grade required
  • Advanced Courses: 80% overall grade required

Check your course syllabus for specific grading breakdown (assignments, quizzes, participation).

Accessing Your Certificates

To view your certificates:

  1. Go to your Student Dashboard
  2. Click Recent Certificates section
  3. All earned certificates are displayed with date issued
  4. Click a certificate to view or download
Verifying Your Certificate

Each certificate includes:

  • Verification Hash: Unique identifier for authenticity
  • QR Code: Scannable code to verify certificate online
  • Issue Date: When you earned the certificate
  • Instructor Signature: Digital verification

To verify a certificate:

  1. Visit the Certificate Verification page
  2. Enter the verification hash or upload the certificate
  3. Scan the QR code with your phone
Sharing Your Achievements

You can share your certificates:

  • Download as PDF for email or printing
  • Share on LinkedIn and other professional networks
  • Include in resumes and job applications
  • Share verification link with employers
Certificates are recognized credentials that demonstrate your completion and competency in course material.
If You Don't Earn a Certificate

If you don't meet the requirements:

  1. Review your grade report
  2. Identify areas that need improvement
  3. Contact your instructor for feedback
  4. You can typically retake the course after a waiting period
  5. Your highest grade will be recorded
